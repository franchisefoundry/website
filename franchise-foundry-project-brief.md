# Franchise Foundry — Project Brief for UK Franchise Market Research

## What is Franchise Foundry?

Franchise Foundry is a UK-based franchise recruitment consultancy founded and run by Ben and Harry. The business exists to fix a fundamental problem in the franchise industry: most brokers and portals are paid by franchisors to fill pipelines, which means their incentives are misaligned with the people they're supposed to be helping — prospective franchisees.

Franchise Foundry operates on a different model. The service to franchisees is completely free. They are still paid by franchisors when a successful match is made, but their approach is franchisee-first: they turn down brands they don't believe in, prepare candidates thoroughly before any introduction, and walk people through the unit economics of a business before they ever meet a franchisor.

The tagline that best captures their position: **"Traditional brokers work for the brand. We work for you."**

---

## Who Runs It

Ben and Harry are both career hospitality operators — not advisors or consultants who have observed the industry from the outside. Between them they have 15+ years of hands-on experience building, operating and scaling real hospitality franchise brands across the UK. Their background includes:

- Launching, operating and growing a 17-site burger chain across the UK
- Franchise development and recruitment for an international hospitality concept
- Building and managing a multi-site Asian food brand

Critically, both still work in hospitality. Franchise Foundry is not a second career — it runs alongside their operational work. This is what keeps their knowledge current and their brand relationships genuine.

---

## What They Do

Franchise Foundry connects prospective franchisees with hospitality franchise brands across the UK. The matching process covers:

1. **Discovery** — understanding what the candidate actually wants: lifestyle, income goals, budget, location, long-term ambition
2. **Matching** — scoring candidates against every brand in their network based on data, not commission availability
3. **Preparation** — walking candidates through unit economics, the realities of each model, and the pressure points before any brand meeting
4. **Introduction and support** — making the right introduction at the right time and staying involved through to franchise award

They are selective about the brands in their network. Brands they don't believe in are turned down.

---

## Their Position in the Market

Franchise Foundry describes itself as the only **franchisee-first** matching service in the UK. They contrast themselves with:

- **Traditional franchise brokers** — incentivised to move volume and get signings across the line, regardless of fit
- **Franchise portals** — paid per click, with no real incentive to find a good match
- **General consultants** — typically without real operational experience on either side of the table

The core competitive advantage is insider knowledge: having sat at the franchisor's recruitment table, they understand what franchisors are actually looking for, where they flex, and where they won't — and they share all of that with the franchisee before any conversation begins.

## Tone and Voice

When writing content for or about Franchise Foundry, the tone should be:

- **Direct and confident** — they have strong opinions and aren't afraid to say the industry is broken
- **Plain-spoken** — no jargon, no broker-speak; they talk like operators, not salespeople
- **Franchisee-first** — every piece of content should reflect whose side they're on
- **British English** — the business is UK-based and all content should use British spelling and phrasing
- **Credible, not self-promotional** — authority comes from experience, not claims

## How This Brief Should Be Used

This document provides context for a market research project focused on UK franchising. Research findings, data and industry trends gathered through this project will be used by Franchise Foundry as the basis for blog content. 

Blog topics may include things like: the state of UK hospitality franchising, what prospective franchisees get wrong, how to evaluate a franchise opportunity, unit economics explained, red flags in franchise agreements, how the broker model works and why it's flawed, trends in food and beverage franchising, and similar themes.

All content should be grounded in the UK market specifically, and should reflect Franchise Foundry's position as operators who have been on both sides of the recruitment table.
